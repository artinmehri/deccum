# Deccum Demo Script (~90 seconds)

## Setup
1. Open the deployed app (or `npm run dev` → http://localhost:3000)
2. Hard refresh once so Demo Mode is cold

## Script

**0:00–0:15 — Problem**
> “Meet Jordan, 52, just left a corporate job. Money is spread across a 401(k), Roth, brokerage, pension, cash, and Social Security. Most people guess which account to tap first — and guessing is expensive.”

Click **Try demo in 90s**.

**0:15–0:35 — Optimized timeline**
> “Deccum doesn’t show balances. It shows the sequence. Age 52: brokerage first — keeps taxable income low and preserves ACA eligibility. No early-withdrawal penalty.”

Click a couple timeline years. Point at the explanation panel.

**0:35–0:55 — Flip to Naive**
Toggle **Naive**.
> “Same person, same money. Naive order drains the 401(k) first — penalties every year, MAGI blows past the ACA threshold.”

**0:55–1:20 — Comparison**
Scroll to **Naive vs optimized**.
> “Optimized cuts roughly $145k of pre-Medicare tax and penalty drag, keeps ACA eligibility for 8 more years, and holds about $210k more at age 65. The order you withdraw money matters.”

**1:20–1:30 — Close**
> “Deccum is a withdrawal sequencing engine — the smallest product that proves that claim.”

## Backup talking points
- Assumptions are explicit and configurable
- No fake brokerage sync — judges see real logic
- AI/explanations describe decisions; the rules engine decides
